## Why I'm Here

AI isn't going anywhere, and anyone who thinks they can work in tech over the next decade without understanding it is going to get left behind. That's not an opinion anymore, it's just the direction everything is moving. Security included.

What drew me to Anthropic's courses specifically wasn't just the certificates — it was the depth. I didn't want a surface-level "here's what AI is" overview. I wanted to actually understand how to work with these tools, connect them to real applications, integrate them into my workflow, and eventually understand how they fit into a cybersecurity context. Anthropic is the company behind Claude, one of the leading AI models right now, and learning from the source means you're getting the real picture, not a watered-down third-party interpretation.

The goal is fluency — not just knowing that AI tools exist but knowing how to actually use them, prompt them effectively, connect them to external services, and build things with them. That's the difference between someone who dabbles and someone who can actually leverage AI as a force multiplier in their work.

---

## The Cybersecurity Connection

This is the part most people in security aren't talking about yet — but they will be soon.

AI is already being integrated into SOC workflows. Threat detection, log summarization, alert triage, writing detection rules, generating incident reports — these are all areas where AI tools are starting to show up in real security environments. The analysts who understand how to work alongside these tools effectively are going to be significantly more productive than those who don't.

There's also a defensive side that's just as important. Understanding how AI models work, how they can be manipulated, where their limitations are, and how attackers might attempt to exploit AI-powered systems — that's an emerging threat surface the security industry is still catching up to. Prompt injection attacks, model manipulation, AI-assisted phishing at scale — these aren't hypothetical anymore.

I'll be upfront — some of what I document here around AI and security intersections may have inaccuracies right now. This is a fast-moving space and best practices are still being written in real time. I'll update these notes as my understanding deepens and as the field matures. The goal isn't to be a definitive resource — it's to document my learning honestly and build on it over time.

## What I'm Most Focused On

The MCP and API courses are where I want to get to. MCP lets you build servers and clients that connect Claude with external services — that's the piece that excites me most. The ability to connect AI to real applications, automate workflows, and build tools that actually do something useful. From a security standpoint, understanding how these integrations work also means understanding how they could be exploited. Every connection point between an AI model and an external service is a potential attack surface.

The AI Fluency courses are the foundation — the 4D framework covers Delegation, Description, Discernment, and Diligence, giving a structured way to think about collaborating with AI effectively and safely. That framework applies directly to how I want to use AI in my day-to-day security work.
