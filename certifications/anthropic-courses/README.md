## The Cybersecurity Connection

This is the part most people in security aren't talking about yet — but they will be soon.

AI is already being integrated into SOC workflows. Threat detection, log summarization, alert triage, writing detection rules, generating incident reports — these are all areas where AI tools are starting to show up in real security environments. The analysts who understand how to work alongside these tools effectively are going to be significantly more productive than those who don't.

There's also a defensive side to this that's just as important. Understanding how AI models work, how they can be manipulated, where their limitations are, and how attackers might attempt to exploit AI-powered systems — that's an emerging threat surface that the security industry is still catching up to. Prompt injection attacks, model manipulation, AI-assisted phishing at scale, deepfakes — these aren't hypothetical anymore. Having foundational AI knowledge isn't just about productivity, it's about understanding the threats that are coming.

Some of what I document here around AI and security intersections may have inaccuracies or gaps right now. This is a fast-moving space and best practices are still being written in real time. What's accurate today might evolve significantly over the next year. I'll update these notes as my understanding deepens and as the field itself matures. The goal isn't to be a definitive resource — it's to document my learning honestly and build on it over time.

## What I'm Most Focused On

The MCP and API courses are where I want to get to. MCP lets you build servers and clients that connect Claude with external services using three core primitives — tools, resources, and prompts. That's the piece that excites me most — the ability to connect AI to real applications, automate workflows, and build tools that actually do something useful rather than just chat.

From a security standpoint, understanding how these integrations work also means understanding how they could be exploited. Every connection point between an AI model and an external service is a potential attack surface. Learning to build these things properly means learning where the vulnerabilities live.

The AI Fluency courses are the foundation — the 4D framework covers Delegation, Description, Discernment, and Diligence, giving a structured way to think about collaborating with AI effectively and safely. That framework applies directly to how I want to use AI in my day-to-day security work.
